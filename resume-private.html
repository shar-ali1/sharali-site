Methodology

How I screen roles — a verification-first job filter

I built Timely AI on one idea — the AI proposes, a human verifies before anything counts. When I started looking for a Summer 2027 AI engineering internship, I pointed the same method at the search itself: instead of applying to everything with “AI” in the title, I built a screen.

The three non-negotiables
Verification culture 01

Does the role or company measure correctness (evals, testing, reliability), or just assume it?

Production stakes 02

Does it ship to real users and real data, not sandbox demos?

AI as core, on consequential actions 03

Is AI the product itself, and does a wrong output carry real cost (a wrong clinical read, a bad calendar write), not just a softer metric miss?

A pre-filter before the three

Those three screen for fit, not for whether I can actually apply. A level / eligibility / timeline / credibility gate runs first, so a perfect-fit role that quietly requires a completed master’s or ten years of experience doesn’t waste screening effort.

What the system caught

A role that passed all three non-negotiables but required a completed master’s — caught by the eligibility gate.

A posting with real AI work but an impossible listing (a salary contradicted by an unpaid-intern question and a “GPA above 4.5” requirement) — caught by the credibility gate.

The refinement

The most useful failure wasn’t in the screening — it was in my criteria. A marketing-automation role built genuinely with LLMs passed “AI as core,” but it wasn’t the work I meant. So I tightened the third non-negotiable to add a stakes clause: AI as core AND the AI takes consequential actions on real data. The criterion grew because a boundary case forced it — the same way my Timely AI eval harness improved when a cancel-intent case failed at 33% and I reworked it to 100%.
